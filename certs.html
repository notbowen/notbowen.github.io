Events & Certificates

NOI 2022

In February 2022, I attended the 25th National Olympiad of Informatics hosted by NUS School of Computing. I was close to a Bronze medal, and I just needed 5 more points to get it :(

However, I had learnt a lot about C++ from preparing for this olympiad, such as different algorithms like binary search and greedy, time complexity, how different data types such as a vector or deque worked and learnt about pointers.

Ngee Ann Amazing Race

My team and I participated in Ngee Ann's Amazing Race: Virtual Hunter 2022. We gathered around 8k points and used them to buy ourselves a School of Infocomm Technology shirt, along with other prizes such as an SD card, a thumb drive and a mousepad.

It was a great experience, and I learned a lot about image metadata and steganography, and how they can be used to hide information :D

CTF.SG

CTF.SG is a Capture the Flag competition focusing on areas such as Web, Reverse Engineering, Cryptography et cetera. My team and I secured 40th place, with a score of 1782. I learnt a lot about how hashes worked and even used MD5 magic hashes to get one of the flags.

NYP YCEP

This was the first CTF I had attended, conducted by NYP, and I finished in the top 15. I had learnt many new skills, such as the existence of robots.txt, basics of web servers, CVEs, steganography and so much more.

CSA-Elastic THC 2021

Threat Hunting Challenge 2021 was a Blue team CTF challenge, organised by CSA and Elastic. It taught me how to use Kibana and Elasticsearch to detect cyber threats, and I am proud to say that I made it to the finals :D

HACK@AC

HACK@AC is a CTF competition organised by ACJC in collaboration with CSA. It consisted of Crypto, Forensics, Pwn, Reverse Engineering, Web and Steganography. My main takeaway from this competition was how image steganography works.

A. YCEP

CSA Singapore organised an Advanced YCEP Bootcamp, where participants did modules on various aspects of cyber-security, such as Operating Systems, Recon, Metasploit et cetera. It deepened my understanding of the Linux operating system and OS basics such as the shell and PATH.

Building BloCS 2021

This was the first computing event that I had attended, and I really improved a lot after going through this event. It taught me the basics of AI using Teachable Machine, and even taught us how to create a Chrome Dino game AI using python's NEAT library!

As a final project, my team and I made a "story telling bot", which used deepai's text generator via their API to generate stories based off user input :D
